About This Site

Hello friends,
This feels awfully similar to writing a tinder bio. And let me tell you, I haven't got much luck on it.
I am a third year computer student at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo. I took a lot of OOP classes, but I guess I always knew that my heart goes for front-end web development. It's always nice to know that you have a lot of freedom to put your imagination to life. Also, you get to see the result of your code right after you type it -- which is also nice.
If I am not on my computer (which is a great anomaly), you can find me at the nearest McDonalds eating chicken nuggets.

Anyway, when I found out that I needed to make a site about llamas, I quickly looked through my planner (because it's January and I'm still trying to be productive), I figured that between doing class projects and attempting to have a social life, I have approximately 10 hours to build this site.



This is how it went:

  • Hour 1: Design Phase.
    I was thinking to challenge myself not to use any framework for this site. But having just built a web app without any framework at all a couple weeks prior, I decided that it was enough to showcase my understanding of HTML, CSS, Javascript. I decided that I will use JQuery to simply the process of manipulating DOM.
  • Hour 2-5: Writing llama-articles.html
    This is the page containing summary, history, behavior, and nutrition of llamas. I decided to include a slider on the history section to show my understanding on manipulating DOM.
  • Hour 6: Writing about.html
  • Hour 7-8:Writing quiz.html
    This is the "meet your llama" section. Similarly, this was also written to show my understanding of DOM and also to show some creativity.
  • Hour 9 - 9.5: re-styling, making sure everything works on web and mobile the way I want them to.
    I almost forgot that making things look pretty take slightly longer than I expect
  • Hour 10: Writing this very page you're currently reading!
